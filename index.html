Hi! I'm Nikhil.

I graduated (online) this May from Duke University, where I studied a mix of Computer Science, Mathematics, and Classics as an A.B. Duke Scholar. I am interested in mathematical finance, mechanism design, and computer vision.

I am moving this August to New York City, where I will be joining a Proprietary Trading Frim as a Quantitative Trader. During college, I spent summers at Jane Street Capital as a Trading intern and at Airbnb as a Software Engineering intern working on an ensemble of machine learning models that return daily price recommendations for listings.

At Duke, I was a part of the Prediction Analysis Lab of Professor Cynthia Rudin. I researched new model architectures for Single-Image Super Resolution (think: CSI zoom-and-enhance), first with convolutional neural networks (CNNs)and then with generative adverserial networks (GANs). For the final year or so, we worked specifically on upscaling pictures of faces using entirely computer-generated recreations. We published and presented papers at NTIRE at CVPR 2018 and at CVPR 2020. You can find those papers here and here.

Outside of the classroom, I was involved with organizing HackDuke for 3 years and helped start and run the Duke Poker Club. I am an avid crossword and sudoku enthusiast and love playing tennis, drums, poker, and other betting games. I am also a huge fan of the Dallas Cowboys, Dallas Mavericks, Florida Gator football, and (of course) Duke Basketball.
